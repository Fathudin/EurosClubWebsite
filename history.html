Early Days

The Polish Football Association was founded in 1919. Poland played its first international match in 1921 against Hungary.

Early Achievements

Poland made a strong impression by reaching the quarter-finals of the 1938 World Cup in France.

Golden Era

1974 World Cup: Poland had a standout performance in the 1974 World Cup in West Germany, finishing in third place. The team, led by stars like Robert Gadocha, defeated Brazil 1-0 in the third-place match.

1978 World Cup: They performed well again in the 1978 World Cup in Argentina, finishing in 7th place.

1982 World Cup: Poland reached the semi-finals of the 1982 World Cup in Spain and ended up in third place. Key players included Zbigniew Boniek.

Recent Years

2016 UEFA Euro: At the UEFA Euro 2016 in France, Poland made it to the quarter-finals but was eliminated by Portugal in a penalty shootout.

2020 UEFA Euro: The tournament was held in 2021 due to COVID-19. Poland was in Group E but did not advance past the group stage.

Notable Players

Robert Lewandowski: A standout forward, Lewandowski is known for his goal-scoring prowess and has been a major player for both the national team and FC Barcelona.

Zbigniew Boniek: A key figure in the 1980s, Boniek was famous for his skill and versatility.

Looking Ahead

Poland continues to be a strong team in international football, with a dedicated fan base and talented players. The team aims to build on its past successes and strive for future achievements.
